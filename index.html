What the F%$^ is PGP?

Pretty Good Privacy (PGP) is a computer program and standard (OpenPGP) for encrypting and decrypting data.

Why does my colleague / friend / relative keep bothering me about it?

Because they are a hero of privacy.

How do I decrypt this message?

If you're not sure what a PGP encrypted message looks like here's an example. 

-----BEGIN PGP MESSAGE-----
Version: GnuPG v2

jA0ECQMCpaoXuEY+v7Hj0joBmBTaMAifhgCkuyaXnOtquT+vcGlleO+BTlzff6If
p7JuoWzpbcgSuhDiDKgZN5FOWqHwTJC829jj
=FeY3
-----END PGP MESSAGE-----

This simplest way to decrypt a PGP message is to use the GNU implementation of PGP, GnuPG. 

gpg -d /path/to/message

How do I encrypt a message?

There are two way to encrypt messages. The first and arguably most secure is to use your recipients public key. Once you've acquired the public key of the person you want to communicate with, you can encrypt your message with the following command: 

gpg -aer public_key_id /path/to/message
